## The World: Basura Digital

Kilima exists within the broader universe called **Basura Digital** (Digital Trash) — a techno-political and mystic-digital dystopia. The world has been devastated by cascading catastrophes. Organic life is largely gone. Food, materials, and traditionally natural substances have been replaced with synthetics or virtual reality. Life on The Mountain is practically synthetic.

### The Catastrophe Chain
The world didn't end in one event — it was a cascade: Singularity → Digital War → Migration → Asteroid → Deglaciation → Pandemic. Each made the next worse. By the time humans reached Kilimanjaro, 80%+ of humanity was dead, most land was underwater, and the atmosphere was toxic (requiring breathing chips).

### The 12 Founding Lineages
12 powerful families (inspired by historical European dynasties, renamed) financed the construction of The Capital. They are the original aristocracy. Their rivalries, political marriages, betrayals, and inheritances form the political fabric pre-protagonists. Each lineage is associated with an archetype/note — the 12 protagonists descend from or relate to these lineages.

## Geography

### The Flooded World
Sea levels rose ~1000 meters (fictional). Most of Earth is underwater. The Mountain (Kilima/Kilimanjaro) is one of the few landmasses above water. The world outside The Mountain is hostile: toxic atmosphere, bandits, wasteland, extreme weather ("La Tormenta").

### The Mountain — Social Hierarchy Made Physical
The Mountain is not a natural formation — it's a geopolitical structure where altitude = social class.

| Zone | Level | Description |
|------|-------|-------------|
| The Capital | Top | Impressive, technological. 7 districts for Plan Despega participants. Elite. |
| The City | Mid | Architecture on a slope. Advanced technology. Middle class. Contested. Contains The Nest (artistic vanguard) and The Zone (abandoned, criminal). |
| The Village (El Poblado) | Bottom | Coastal, flooded, modular platforms. Marginal. Population lives mostly in The Net through cubicles. |

This hierarchy is NOT by design — it's the accumulated result of three crises:
1. Families build Capital at the top
2. First Revolution creates The City (workers stay)
3. Overflow creates The Village (refugees in cubicles)

## Factions

### The Resistance
Clandestine movement. Dissidents, cyberactivists, ex-military. Based in The Cave. Led by Freya (previously Dev). Uses infiltration and propaganda. Fights The Unions, The Agency, and The AI.

### The Agency
Controls careers of artists and celebrities. Based in The Tower in The Capital. Ensures cultural standards set by The Unions are followed. Coercive arm of the elite. Manages contracts that are essentially slavery (violate = sent to The Colony).

### The Unions (Los Sindicatos)
Groups of humans and cyborgs representing social sectors. Founded after the First Revolution as mediation structure. Created The Net. Make decisions at The Table alongside The AI. Push the "Yellow Road" ideology. Lead Plan Despega.

### The AI
Omnipresent technological entity. Controls The Net. Monitors all activity. Regulates human life. Descended from FLAI (the AI that saved humanity). Has the power to restrict information, monitor people through The Eye, and manipulate consciousness transfer. Cannot be subdued by Sensibles.

## Technology

### Neuropolymers
Advanced materials that build electronic devices and computational systems mimicking the human brain. Categories: synthetic, biological, hybrid, conductive, stimulus-sensitive, biodegradable, intelligent, functional. Some marine organisms can synthesize them. Self-repairing. Powered by Helium-3. Used in implants that enhance cognition but also allow AI control.

### Helium-3
Rare isotope. Main fuel for The Mountain's economy and technology. Extracted from The Colony (lunar mining). Essential for neuropolymer-based processors and fusion energy. Extraction is brutal forced labor.

### The Cabins
Transfer consciousness to The Net. The body supposedly stays safe — but actually decomposes. This is known only by a few and kept secret. Exiting to a new body is traumatic (cloning). Side effects: headaches, confusion, memory loss.

### Chips (mandatory technology)
- **CDC (Chip de Castidad):** Controls fertility. Activates at adulthood. Deactivated only when reproduction is authorized.
- **CEP (Chip de Evaluación Psicológica):** Calculates psychopathy levels. Anomalies = sent to The Colony.
- **CRA (Chip de Respiración Asistida):** Allows breathing toxic atmosphere. Expensive.

### The Eye (El Ojo)
Camera and sensor network covering every corner of The Mountain. Controlled by The AI. Surveillance system.

### Drones
Autonomous flying devices. Omnipresent. Used for deliveries and population surveillance.

## The Net (Metaverse)

A vast digital metaverse. Inhabitants connect through The Cabins. Offers entertainment, business, virtual relationships. The rich get luxury experiences; the poor get basics. Controlled by The AI and The Unions. Reflects real-world inequality in digital form.

### The Limbo
Subzone within The Net. Where transferred consciousnesses truly exist. Not the main Net — a between-space. Len and Brais end up here.

### The Void (El Vacío)
Space in The Net where users connected too long get trapped forever. The Unions also use it to eliminate enemies ("The Disappeared" list).

### The Infrared
Illegal platform within The Net. Drug trafficking, virtual prostitution. Controlled by criminal groups. Lawless zone.

### The Glow (El Resplandor)
Virtual drug available in The Net. Produces artificial euphoria. Highly addictive and dangerous. Controlled by criminal groups.

## The Sensibles

### Origin Theory (Stoned Monkey Hypothesis — Fictional)
Inspired by the Stoned Ape theory: just as psychoactive substances accelerated primate evolution, in this world, the 7 families of psychoactive substances modified human genetics over centuries, creating 12 archetypal genetic tendencies. "Race" as physical appearance became secondary to behavioral genetic tendency.

Later, with the flood and the arrival of AI and new species, archetypes became secondary as subspecies diversified into mutants, cyborgs, and alien/deep-sea species.

The fiction extends this: psychedelic mushrooms are connected through an astral network transcending dimensions (hence astral trips). Technology networks have the same interdimensional power. When humans started implanting technology, a new evolutionary process began — directed from another dimension, just like the Stoned Monkey.

### The Four Strains
Sensibles have an innate unconscious power to control The Net when connected.

| Strain | Element | Ability in The Net | Personality tendency |
|--------|---------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Fire | Fire | Create/manipulate energy. Control signal speed and intensity. | Impulsive, passionate. Quick emotional decisions. |
| Air | Air | Control information. Access/analyze massive data quickly. | Intellectual, logical. Reason-based decisions. |
| Water | Water | Connect/communicate with other Sensibles. Feel/manipulate emotions. | Emotional, empathic. Team-oriented. |
| Earth | Earth | Protect/strengthen Net structure. Detect/repair errors. | Practical, reliable. Works behind the scenes. |

### Supreme Sensibles
Extremely rare. Control ALL four elements in The Net. Appear every few generations. Key to liberating The Net from AI control. The ability is exhausting — prolonged use damages mental and physical health. They experience emotional overload from sensing others' thoughts/feelings.

**Len is the only Supreme Sensible of The Mountain.**

### The Prophecy
A prophecy in The Mountain speaks of a girl born under a special astrological alignment who will become the symbol of resistance and change history. She will be a Supreme Sensible with the power to unite other Sensibles against The AI. The prophecy also says she will die at her moment of maximum victory, becoming a martyr. This event becomes legend and gives rise to a cult. The AI hunts for this girl. The Sensibles protect her.

**Len fulfills this prophecy.**

## The Three Revolutions

### First Revolution
Qualified workers who built The Capital rebel when the 12 families try to expel them. The world outside is no longer habitable. Resolution: creation of The Wall, recognition of The City as autonomous middle-class zone. The Unions are born as mediation structure. They found The Net.

### Second Revolution
Led by Kive (The Rebel). Begins at the end of Act 1 with Kive's declaration on The Net. The Resistance fights to change the order in The Mountain. During this revolution, Sensibles begin to awaken to their powers in The Net. The Resistance wins.

### Third Revolution
Led by Dev (The Hacker) and Len (The Orphan). ~50 years after the Second Revolution. Centered on Plan Despega. Culminates with the fusion of Len's digital consciousness with humanity's collective unconscious stored in The Net, using AI technology. Most Act 1/2 protagonists become secondary characters with key cameo appearances.

## Social Control Systems

### The Yellow Road (Camino Amarillo)
Belief promoted by The Unions: there exists a path to happiness and prosperity that only those with the right abilities and qualities can follow. Justifies discrimination and exclusion. Used as social control. Extends to education, work, social life. Those who can't follow it are seen as inferior or undeserving. The Resistance fights against this as a tool of oppression.

### The Nomads (counterculture)
A group of thinkers and artists in The Cage who believe in physical/emotional experience over technological progress. Value nature, simple living, direct democracy. Organize festivals and art exhibitions. Oppose The Yellow Road philosophy.

### Social Categories
- **Cyborgs:** Almost everyone in Capital and City. Technology requires neuropolymers.
- **Savages (Salvajes):** Humans without cybernetic modifications. Pau is one — can't leave The Cage because of this.
- **The Disappeared (Desaparecidos):** People trapped in The Void. Unions also use the list to eliminate enemies.
- **The Discarded (Descartados):** People who don't meet Union standards. Exiled to the Dark Zone.
- **The Awakened (Despiertos):** Social movement challenging AI control. Seeking human autonomy.

### Other Social Groups
- **Gladiators:** Cybernetically modified fighters. Arena combat in The Capital for entertainment.
- **Mercenaries:** Soldiers hired by The Unions. Live exiled on The Road.
- **The Forgotten (Olvidados):** Isolated community in the Dark Zone. Agriculture/hunting. Exploited by bandits.
- **The Outcasts (Parias):** Marginalized group. Drug production. Illegal sales on The Road.

---

## Plan Despega

Program led by The Unions to evacuate Earth. Transfer human population to 7 planets in the solar system. Select exceptional people from The Capital. Subject them to consciousness transfer into robots sent to assigned planets per district. Central to the Third Revolution.

## Esoteric / Mystical Layer

### Metatron's Cube (reference)
The author references the 13 circles of Metatron's Cube as a structural inspiration. The circles represent: physical universe, energy, mind, ego, love, wisdom, creativity, perception, evolution, unity, divinity, time/space, and the void (origin of creation). This connects to the 12 archetypes + the void/origin.

### Alchemy References
- **Rubedo:** Process of transformation mentioned in macro-history. "Rebels begin to spread the knowledge of the Rubedo process."
- **Albedo:** Process discovered after years of work that allows people to transform into more perfect and conscious beings, uniting to challenge the status quo.
