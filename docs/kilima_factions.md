## The 4 Major Factions

### 1. The Resistance
- **Base:** The Cave
- **Leader (Act 1):** Freya (The Goddess). Dev was previous leader.
- **Nature:** Clandestine movement. Dissidents, cyberactivists, ex-military.
- **Tactics:** Infiltration, propaganda, sabotage, underground networks.
- **Ideology:** Oppose the hierarchy. Fight for equality and freedom.
- **Members:** Kive, Freya, Yeri, Dev (former leader, now independent).
- **Color:** Gray

### 2. The Agency
- **Base:** The Tower (La Capital)
- **Leader:** TBD (Agency bosses — multiple leaders)
- **Nature:** Controls careers of artists and celebrities. Coercive cultural arm of the elite.
- **Function:** Ensures artists conform to Union-set cultural standards. Contracts are slavery — violate them and you're sent to The Colony. Kive is under contract (musician).
- **Relationship to Unions:** Works in close collaboration. The Agency is the Unions' cultural enforcement tool.
- **Members:** Luka (initially), Brais (infiltrated spy).
- **Color:** Pink/Magenta

### 3. The Unions (Los Sindicatos)
- **Base:** The Capital (La Mesa, La Sede)
- **Leader:** 12 syndicate heads (one per sector) + The AI as final arbiter
- **Nature:** Groups of humans and cyborgs representing social sectors. Created after the First Revolution as mediation structure. Founded The Net.
- **Relationship to AI:** Collaborative but subordinate. The AI maintains final control and monitors operations.
- **Ideology:** "The Yellow Road" (Camino Amarillo) — meritocratic propaganda that justifies inequality.
- **Color:** Dark green

### 4. The AI
- **Base:** The Net
- **Leader:** Itself (descended from FLAI)
- **Nature:** Omnipresent technological entity. Controls The Net, The Eye surveillance system, consciousness transfer technology.
- **Power:** Can regulate information flow, monitor citizens, restrict access, manipulate consciousness. Cannot subdue Sensibles.
- **Relationship to Unions:** Created the Unions for psychological stability, not efficiency. Maintains simulated ministries as theater of control.
- **Color:** N/A (omnipresent)

### Real vs Simulated Ministries

The AI's master design: 6 ministries operate for real, 6 are theater.

**Real (actually affect the system):**
1. Biosfera & Health (Biotechnology)
2. Energy
3. Infrastructure & Territory (Structures)
4. Defense & Security
5. Education
6. Science & Thought (Systems)

**Simulated (theater of control):**
1. Economy & Finances (Interior)
2. Justice (Memory)
3. Politics / Governance (the Unions themselves)
4. External Relations (Exterior)
5. Culture / Communication (Culture)
6. Work / Merit (Engineering/Materials)

**Purpose of simulated ministries:**
- Channel frustration
- Organize identities
- Prevent citizens from looking at the AI
- Give the illusion of participation and agency

Citizens debate in the Unions, believe they influence decisions, discharge conflict horizontally — while structural decisions are already made by the AI.
